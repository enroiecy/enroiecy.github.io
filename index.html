Object Detection Model

For my senior capstone project at Pepperdine, we were given the freedom to choose any project that demonstrated our skills in programming. I thought this would be the perfect opportunity to engineer a solution to a problem within my own community. I worked alongside two of my colleagues and we created an object detection model that identifies open spots at our university's parking lots.

The goal of the project was to display the number of parking spots open based on a live video stream of the lots. It would make the lives of those who commuted easier and if it was one of those days where there was no parking, they could stay home and possibly attend zoom.

We ended up selling our project to Pepperdine's IT team where they took over to fully implement it into the Pepperdine app. I hope what we created can become a reality for the students and make their lives easier. If not, I hope this raised the issue of parking on a bigger scale and they would view it as an immediate concern.

American Roulette Simulator

In this project, the goal was to document 1000 sucessive bets to analyze the Martingale strategy. The strategy involves doubling the bet every time the bet misses to cover the amount from the previous loss. All scenarios were run with the notion that every bet was going to be placed on black and the results were visually presented. After analysis of the charts, I concluded that I should not be going to Vegas to play roulette with my current bankroll.
